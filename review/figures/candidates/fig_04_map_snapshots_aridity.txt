Figure 4 (aridity variant) — FLUXNET network growth on CGIAR Aridity Index backdrop

Same four-panel layout and site lists as the white-background variant. The
backdrop shows the CGIAR Global Aridity Index v3.1 (Zomer et al. 2022) binned
into five UNEP aridity classes (shared across all four panels):

  Hyper-Arid    (AI < 0.05)   #d73027 (red)
  Arid          (AI 0.05–0.20) #fc8d59 (orange)
  Semi-Arid     (AI 0.20–0.50) #ffff33 (yellow)
  Dry Sub-Humid (AI 0.50–0.65) #66bd63 (green)
  Humid         (AI ≥ 0.65)   white (no colour)

The aridity backdrop illustrates the strong sampling bias of the FLUXNET
network toward temperate-humid climates across all four datasets, and how
arid and hyper-arid regions remain consistently underrepresented.

Raw AI values stored as integers (AI × 10000); scaling factor 0.0001 applied
per Zomer et al. (2022). Raster aggregated to ~0.2-degree resolution for
display. Country outlines (Natural Earth 1:50m) rendered above the aridity
layer. Aridity legend collected at the figure bottom.

Panels labelled "(comparison — non-Shuttle)" use historical site lists that
are not part of the primary Shuttle-based analysis (see CLAUDE.md §1).
